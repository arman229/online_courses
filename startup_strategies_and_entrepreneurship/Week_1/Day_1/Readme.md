# Difference Between a Startup and a Business

**Business:** Any activity done to earn profit by selling products or services.
**Startup:** A type of business that focuses on innovation and fast growth.

| Business                  | Startup                            |
| ------------------------- | ---------------------------------- |
| Main goal is profit       | Main goal is growth + profit later |
| Uses proven ideas         | Uses new or improved ideas         |
| Grows slowly and steadily | Grows very fast                    |
| Low to medium risk        | High risk                          |
| Usually self-funded       | Often funded by investors          |

---

# When is a Business Called a Startup?

A business is called a startup when it:

* Solves a problem in a new way
* Can scale to many users quickly
* Is designed for rapid growth

**Note:** All startups are businesses, but not all businesses are startups.

---

# What is a Product-Based Company and a Service-Based Company?

**Product-Based Company:** Makes a product once and sells it to many customers.

**Examples:**

* Mobile apps
* Software tools (Photoshop)
* Physical products (mobile phones, laptops)

**Service-Based Company:** Earns money by doing work for clients repeatedly.

**Examples:**

* Software houses
* Freelancing services
* Consulting services

**Note:** Product companies build once and sell many times. Service companies work every time they earn.

---

# Who is an Entrepreneur?

An **entrepreneur** is a person who:

* Gets an idea
* Takes risks
* Starts a business
* Manages failures
* Keeps improving

---

# What is Entrepreneurship?

**Entrepreneurship** is the act of starting something new, taking risks, and turning an idea into a business to solve a problem and earn money.

**Note:** Entrepreneurship is the **process or journey**.

> An entrepreneur does entrepreneurship to build a startup (or any business).

---

# Who is an Investor?

An **investor** is a person or company that gives money to a business in exchange for profit or ownership.

---

# Who is a Stakeholder?

A **stakeholder** is a person or group that is affected by or has an interest in a business.

**Example:** If your startup succeeds:

* Employees get salaries
* Investors get returns
* Customers get the product

All of them are stakeholders.
